## Overview

This study investigates ground-level ozone (O₃) pollution across three air-quality
monitoring stations in the Xiang'an District of Xiamen, China, using measured data from
**2019–2022**. The work characterizes how O₃ forms and behaves over time and examines its
interaction with precursor compounds (NO₂) and fine particulate matter (PM2.5, PM10).

### Goals
- Identify the general patterns governing O₃ formation (hourly, monthly, seasonal, and annual variation).
- Study the interaction between O₃ and other air pollutants across the three stations.
- Analyze three specific pollution episodes in detail to understand local formation versus
  pollutant transport from other regions.

## Key Findings

- O₃-8hr concentration **peaked in September–October** and was **lowest in June**.
- Seasonal and monthly variation were consistent, driven by the meteorological patterns of
  southern China — **temperature and relative humidity** were the dominant factors in ozone formation.
- HYSPLIT trajectories showed that **atmospheric transport of pollutants** from inland urban
  areas and from the sea contributed significantly to Xiamen's ozone levels, on top of locally
  generated O₃ — pointing to an urgent need for regional pollution-control measures.
